Welcome to KarinWeekly.com!

I’m Karin. I’ve been playing piano for 50 years and teaching piano for 22 years. I’d be happy to teach you how to play music, too. I’ve developed a powerful toolbox of teaching skills and materials over the years, including a fundamental lesson backbone that combines playing by ear with reading music. A wide array of musical experiences have gone into making me a music teacher. Over the years, I’ve developed key pillars of my music teaching philosophy upon which I always stay focused.

Key pillars of music teaching philosophy

Discovery is the foundation of learning Audiation is the foundation of music study
  1. Frequently asking questions of the student
  2. Directing the student’s attention without giving away answers
  3. Allowing the student time to think and work through ideas
  4. Following the student’s unique paths of interest
  5. Identifying the student’s personal skills and helping the student reach full potential
  1. Presenting concepts by sound before sight
  2. Teaching ear training from the very first lesson
  3. Intertwining ear training into every other musical concept, including note-reading
  4. Teaching scales by the sound relationships among tones
  5. Using apps and workbooks designed for ear training
  6. Incorporating ear training activities such as: musical dictation, play what you hear, and sight-singing
